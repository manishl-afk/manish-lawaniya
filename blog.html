AI in Audit

5 Ways AI Is Changing SMSF Audit Work

4 min read·AI in Audit

A practical look at where generative AI genuinely helps SMSF audit teams — and where it still doesn't belong.

Read full article →

Most conversations about "AI in audit" jump straight to automation replacing reviewers. That's not what's actually happening on the ground. After integrating generative AI and no-code tools into parts of SMSF audit work, here's where it genuinely earns its place:

1. First-draft documentation. Drafting compliance notes and standard report sections is repetitive, not judgement-heavy. AI drafts, a human edits.

2. Pattern spotting in large data sets. Flagging unusual transactions for a human to look at — not deciding they're a problem.

3. No-code workflow stitching. Connecting tools that don't talk to each other natively, without writing custom software.

4. Excel formula and template generation. Faster template iteration, still tested manually before use.

5. Knowledge transfer. Turning ad-hoc tribal knowledge into documented, repeatable process steps new associates can actually follow.

What hasn't changed: the final compliance sign-off is still a human decision. AI removes drafting time, not accountability.

Excel Automation

Building Excel Tools That Actually Get Used

3 min read·Excel Automation

Lessons from building audit automation templates that audit teams adopt instead of quietly ignoring.

Read full article →

I've built more Excel tools that got abandoned than I'd like to admit early in my career — usually because they were designed around how I thought the process should work, not how associates actually worked.

What changed the outcome: building the tool while doing the task myself, not after stepping back from it. A template built by someone reviewing workpapers gets the small details right — the column someone always has to scroll to find, the formula that breaks when a row gets inserted, the naming convention people actually remember.

The result isn't a flashier spreadsheet. It's one that survives contact with a real audit deadline, gets used without a training session, and reduces review questions instead of creating new ones.

SMSF Compliance

What Makes a Workpaper "Audit-Ready"

3 min read·SMSF Compliance

The documentation habits that separate a smooth SMSF audit from a stressful one.

Read full article →

"Audit-ready" gets used loosely. In practice, it means a reviewer can follow the file without asking the preparer a single clarifying question. A few habits that consistently get a workpaper there:

Consistent structure across files. If every file is organised differently, every review starts from zero.

Source documents linked, not just referenced. "See bank statement" is not the same as the bank statement being attached and tied to the figure it supports.

Reconciliations finished, not started. A reconciliation with an unexplained variance is a flag, not a finished workpaper.

Notes written for someone who wasn't there. If a note only makes sense to the person who wrote it, it isn't documentation.

None of this is exciting. It's also the entire difference between a same-day review and a week of back-and-forth.
